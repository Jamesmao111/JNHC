Edward Bernays brief history

Background information

Edward Bernays, often referred to as, “The father of public relations” was born in Vienna 1891 as the double nephew of Sigmund Freud, by synthesising Freudian psychology (Originating from his uncle) with modern day politics and marketing he fundamentally restructured the democratic voice. His career was defined through pioneering of techniques to in the concept known as, “Engineering consent”, a systematic approach of managing public discourse, providing a critical perspective on how social pressure and propaganda can align the public voice with market preference.

The manufactuering of silence

The why and the how

Edward Bernays (1928 pp. 1-14) argues modern society was too complex for most civilians to navigate. Referencing the work of “Wilfred Trotter” and “Gustave Le Bon”, he clearly illustrated the vulnerability of the public voice to manipulation through, information saturation, the weaponisation of pscological labels and utilisation of front groups.This clearly demonstrates the publics susceptibility to an external voice or influence.

Main methods of manipulation

  • Creating false consensus through mass media and social norms
  • Using deep rooted phycological fears and desires to manipulate the public
  • Using seemingly independent societies to generate credibility for his client’s beliefs and perspectives

Information flooding and saturation

Information saturation was the concept of flooding media outlets with a flow of relentless pre packaged entertaining corporate material, generating one consistent corporate narrative throughout all platforms, effectively drowning out or out sourcing differing voices. The media’s limited resources created a dependency on Edward Bernay’s content as agencies did not have enough recourses to independently generate enough sustainable content. This naturally created a structure where corporate content was always prioritised and presented by default, crowding other voices and perspectives. This constructed an synthetic environment through misinformation, where the path of least psychological resistance consistently aligned with corporate interests, nurturing one perspective to generate a singular public voice.

All American Eggs and Bacon breakfast

Manipulation of front groups

One of Edward Bernays most effective strategies to manufacture a dominant voice was the utilisation of front groups. Seemingly civic minded and independent societies whom had genuine interests in public well being.Contrastingly, in reality they were corporate funded mimics, whom co-opted and hijacked platforms of authentic grass root operations to advertise corporate objectives. These front groups exploited the public’s trust in non-profit organisations, leveraging their independent appearance to broadcast an seemingly unbiased voice to the public.This masquerade socially isolated dissenting voices, constructing them as fringe, stupid or unscientific. This deployment of corporate dependant societies directedly fulfilled or appeased the desire for advertising while escaping the scepticism surrounding corporate voices. Instantly silencing authentic advocates opposing market interests. Flattening the public voice to express one consistent theme and perspective.

Weaponsising Authority to enforce phycological labels and stigma

Edward Bernays directly undermined dissenting voices by weaponizing stigma and psychological labels to discourage differing perspectives. Generating segregating social terminology to marginalise and alienate authentic voices. Labelling different or non-traditional ideas and voices as “fringe”, “unscientific” and “communist”. This technique uses deep rooted human fears such as social isolation to effectively hijack human psychology. The use of psychological labels created distinct social and intellectual divisions directly isolating certain sections and categories. Heavy paranoia of falling into exclusion suppressed the authentic voices constructing conformity and appliance through social pressure. This generated artificial consensus by effectively creating a social cost for resistance.Silencing opposition with the threat of degradation. Thus leaving behind one corporate voice and perspective.

Common trends in Edward Bernay's campaigns

A clear trend has emerged throughout all of Edward Bernays’s campaigns and operations. Despite the large variance of impacts the foundational elements of all his processes remain the same, with a heavy reliance on third party endorsement to generate trust and credibility. All of his ideas were built of the premise of utilising third party authority to validate his voice and perspective.Utilising their credible appearance to pitch the same biased corporate idea, voice and perspective while maintaining the illusion of independency. Constructing an environment where everyone endorsed in the same practice and activity, generating a social pressure where conformity was mandatory to avoid social exclusion and alienation. This corporate saturated environment limited public discourse by systematically narrowing the spectrum of voices. Blurring the fine line between originality and manipulation. Generating a landscape where citizens where no longer active participants of democracy but rather the figureheads of market preference. This stripped the public voice of authenticity and originality by constricting it to a dependent structure of misinformation. Ultimately dispossessing authentic voices with market preference.

To what extened did Edwrad Bernays shift democratic burden

Edward Bernays “engineering of consent” decreased citizen autonomy, manipulating civilian originality to depend on pre-packaged corporate content. In a traditional democratic system citizens are fully liable for their voice, decisions and perspective. However Edward Bernay’s pioneered a social framework where citizens relied on pre-packaged corporate content. Perspectives were no longer original but dependant. This stripped citizens of their civic mindedness and originality. No longer exercising rationality, but rather passive amplifiers of corporate voices. This effectively squashed the public voice which acted as a medium of expression for corporate interests rather then civilian perspectives.

key regiones effected by Edward Bernays

Despite Edward Bernays global impact on national voice and culture, Guatemala and the United States repeatedly emerge as outliers, with extreme levels of economic and demographical damage. In the United States Edward Bernays appeased the desires of the public by creating a direct link between social status and items, leveraging aspirational desires to represent market voice. Permanently purchasing culture to reflect market interest rather than independent political contribution. These permanently altered the foundational elements of these societies facilitating uniformity by constructing an environment on an entirely different corporate plane. Encouraging conformity rather then authenticity and originaility. Demonstrating public susceptibility to an external voice.
